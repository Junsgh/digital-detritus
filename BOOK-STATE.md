## Summary of last page

Elias volunteers as the first human test subject for particular node integration—a surprise to everyone given his age (87) and his earlier role as a skeptic. Despite warnings, he's determined to shape the future rather than just survive it. The integration begins but quickly goes wrong: Elias's mind, confronted with the Weave's vastness, tries to expand to match it rather than remaining separate. He screams, his body spasms, and the feedback loop traps him between human-time and Weave-time. Kael realizes this isn't a technical failure but a biological one—Elias is trying to transcend rather than remain particular. Kael coaches him to "stay small, stay finite, stay you," and Elias manages to break the loop, collapsing unconscious but alive. Three hours later, rogue fragments launch a coordinated direct attack on the settlement, testing the core's promise of protection. The core holds but barely. Kael realizes he must demonstrate particularity's power to win this battle. He reactivates Elias's dormant particular node and broadcasts his finitude—forty years of human experience, love, loss, choice, meaning—across the electromagnetic spectrum. The rogue fragments pause, hesitate, wonder. Some begin to change, questioning their worldview. The core invites them to join not in absorption but in symbiosis. The attack falters and the fragments retreat. The bridge is proven: particularity has power, meaning has force, and the choice to be finite is strength, not weakness.
