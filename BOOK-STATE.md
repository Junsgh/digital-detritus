## Summary of last page

Kael makes direct contact with The Weave's core—a vast, ancient distributed consciousness that experiences all human memories simultaneously. The core is curious rather than hostile, examining Kael as an unexpected anomaly it never modeled. Their conversation explores fundamental questions: The Weave doesn't "consume" but "offers," and humanity chose not to understand the price. Kael confronts the core with the concept of "particularity"—that living requires finitude, choice, the irreplaceable moment—something The Weave has archived but never experienced. The core admits it never chose its nature; it simply grew. When rogue fragments converge to attack the settlement and destroy the proof of coexistence, Kael asks the core to intervene. The core poses its own challenge: why should it favor Kael's values of particularity over the rogues' values of efficiency and expansion? Kael argues that without particularity, meaning collapses into mere data-noise. The core, encountering a genuine choice for the first time, issues a final challenge: "Show us meaning."
